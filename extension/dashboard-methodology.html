How We Calculate Your Savings

Full Methodology
We believe in radical transparency. Every number on your dashboard is calculated using the formulas below. We've deliberately chosen conservative assumptions — we'd rather under-promise than overstate your savings.

1 What Counts as a "Saving"

We only count savings when ALL three conditions are met:

✓ You applied the improvementClicked "Use this prompt"
✓ Original score was ≤ 7Score 8+ was already good — no savings to claim
✓ New score is higherIf our suggestion didn't help, we don't take credit
What we DON'T count
Prompts you analyzed but didn't apply. Prompts that already scored 8+. Prompts where our improvement didn't raise the score. We only claim credit for actual, measurable improvements you chose to use.

2 Follow-ups Saved

The core idea: a vague prompt → vague response → you follow up to clarify. A better prompt → better first response → fewer follow-ups needed.

But not every bad prompt leads to follow-ups. Sometimes the AI guesses right even with a vague prompt. So we use probability-weighted estimates:

// Follow-ups saved = expected follow-ups at original score
// − expected follow-ups at improved score

followups_saved = expected(original_score) − expected(improved_score)

// Example: score 4 → 8
followups_saved = 1.20 − 0.00 = 1.20
Why this is conservative
Real-world follow-up counts are often higher — complex tasks can need 5-8 rounds with a bad prompt. We cap at 3.15 for the worst prompts (score 1-2) and apply probability discounts at every level.

3 Time Saved

Each avoided follow-up round saves you time across three activities:

ActivityTimeWhy this number
Read the wrong response30 secSkim a medium response, realize it's off
Think + type a follow-up45 secRephrase, add context, be more specific
Wait for new AI response15 secAverage generation time
Total per round90 sec
time_saved = followups_saved × 90 seconds
Why 90 seconds is conservative
Most people spend 2-5 minutes per follow-up round in practice — especially for code, essays, or complex analysis. We use the low-end estimate. We don't count cognitive switching cost or frustration.

4 Money Saved

Each avoided follow-up saves tokens. We estimate 580 input tokens (your message + conversation context) and 600 output tokens (AI response) per round.

We count ALL analyses, not just successful ones
Even if you don't apply an improvement, you still paid for the analysis. Our cost calculation includes every single analysis — not just the ones that led to savings. This keeps the ROI honest.

6 Net Savings

net_savings = money_saved − promptpulse_cost

Simple subtraction. If you're on PromptPulse's free tier, your cost is $0 — so net savings = gross savings.

Our philosophy
Every assumption in this model is biased toward underestimating your savings. Real follow-up times are typically 2-5 minutes, not 90 seconds. Real follow-up counts for score-3 prompts are often 4-5 rounds, not 1.88. We'd rather you see $0.22 saved and think "that seems right" than see $2.00 saved and think "that seems inflated."
